About Me

My journey into software development began in high school when I took an introductory programming course at my brother's suggestion. I had always been passionate about the internet — particularly gaming — but that course was the first time I truly explored coding. The puzzle-like challenges and problem-solving aspects immediately clicked with me, setting me on the path to earn a Computer Science degree at George Mason University.

After college, I briefly led development on a startup idea with fellow graduates, but the COVID-19 pandemic quickly put an end to that venture. My professional career took off at Misbar, where I honed my foundational skills. However, due to company-wide layoffs on the U.S. side, I moved on to NurseTim, where I became a key contributor and expanded my expertise. Following a business acquisition, I transitioned to Wolters Kluwer, where I continue to grow — mentoring, taking on larger responsibilities, and deepening my experience with complex and distributed systems.
