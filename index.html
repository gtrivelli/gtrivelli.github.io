About Me

My software journey began in high school when I took an introductory programming course. I had a passion for all things internet, particularly gaming, but had never dived deeper, until that course. The blend of puzzle-like challenges and technology perfectly resonated with me. Naturally, this led to my pursuit of a CS degree when I began studying at George Mason University.

Post-college, I briefly led development on a startup idea with fellow graduates. However, with the onset of the COVID pandemic, that quickly derailed. My professional career truly began at Misbar, where I honed my skills and further built on my foundation of best practices. Shortly after, for non-performance related reasons, the entire U.S. side of the company was let go. I then joined NurseTim, where I grew to be a valuable and knowledgeable contributor. My employment continues at Wolters Kluwer due to a business acquisition. I continue to thrive here; mentoring, continuing to take on larger roles, and increasing my exposure to larger systems.
