About Me

My passion for software development was sparked in high school when I took an introductory programming course at my brother's suggestion. While I had always been fascinated by the internet — especially gaming — this course introduced me to the world of coding. The thrill of solving complex problems and tackling puzzle-like challenges resonated deeply with me, inspiring me to pursue a Computer Science degree at George Mason University.

After graduating, I briefly led the development of a startup project with fellow graduates, but the COVID-19 pandemic brought that venture to an early close. My professional journey began at Misbar, where I built a strong foundation in software development. Following company-wide layoffs on the U.S. side, I joined NurseTim, where I became a key contributor and significantly expanded my skill set. After NurseTim's acquisition, I transitioned to Wolters Kluwer, where I continue to grow by mentoring others, taking on greater responsibilities, and deepening my expertise in complex, distributed systems.
